#Header: Introduction to Derivation
#Content: Derivation is the process of finding the rate at which a function changes. It helps us analyze functions and understand their behavior. Derivation plays a crucial role in mathematics, physics, economics, and many other fields.

#Slide: 5
#Header: The Derivative Formula
#Content: The derivative of a function is the slope of the tangent line at any given point on the graph. The derivative can be found using the formula:

f'(x) = lim(h->0) [f(x+h) - f(x)] / h

#Slide: 6
#Header: Rules of Differentiation
#Content: Differentiation has several rules that make finding derivatives easier, such as the Power Rule, Product Rule, Chain Rule, and Quotient Rule. These rules allow us to find the derivative of complex functions by breaking them down into simpler parts.

#Slide: 7
#Header: Applications of Derivation
#Content: Derivation has various applications in different fields. In physics, it helps determine velocity, acceleration, and motion. In economics, it helps analyze cost, revenue, and profit functions. In engineering, it assists with optimization and modeling, among other applications.

#Header: Common Mistakes to Avoid
#Content: When working with derivation, it's easy to make mistakes. Some common errors include not simplifying expressions, misapplying differentiation rules, and forgetting to use the chain rule when necessary. Let's avoid these mistakes to ensure accurate results.
